About Me: Where Am I Now? Data Science

In my senior year of high school, I took a course called Data Management, which included topics that covered probability theory, and basic regressions/analysis. For my cumulative project in that class, I conducted research on the relationship between a country's GDP per capita, and cereal consumption per capita - which I was interested in after reading the book 'Guns, Germs, and Steel'. As the book outlines, there appears to be a positive correlation between the two variables:

Upon taking this course, I became enticed by the field of data science. However, I was still new to the area, and so during my first and second year of college, I experimented with different subjects - math, chemistry, computer science, etc. However, my passion for data persisted and I ultimately declared as a Data Science major this spring. Data Science is a special joint-major that is offered by the statistics and computer science department at Columbia that was created in response to the growing significance of "big data".
